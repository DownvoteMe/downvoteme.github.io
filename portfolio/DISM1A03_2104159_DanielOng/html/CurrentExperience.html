My life in Singapore Polytechnic.

SP's computer rooms
The building
SP classroom

The main difference between secondary school life and Singapore Polytechnic is very noticeable. Instead of basic subjects like Mathematics, Chemistry and English, SP teaches specialized subjects that cater to the aspirations of every student. Students who enroll into Singapore Polytechnic and want to go for a specific course that helps in their career can do so, and will have lessons specialized to their fields of interest. For example, as I have much interest in programming, I joined Singapore Polytechnic's School of Computing, which has subjects that build skills essential to programming in the working life. The timetable below shows a template of what I learn every day.

Singapore Polytechnic was a breath of fresh air compared to school life before. It was perfect for someone like me who wanted to hone their own talents without needing to juggle academic subjects along with it. Singapore Polytechnic not only gave me a chance to improve what I do best in through lessons, but also gives a lot of free time in their timetable to let me work on my personal projects. To top it off, the general athmosphere of the campus is constructed to be relaxing, keeping us motivated while we study. With helpful teachers and informative lessons, I would say that Singapore Polytechnic is a dream come true for my academic life.

If you want to enroll into a local Polytechnic, you should first consider your options in the future. If you already have a talent you want to build upon, Polytechnics provide the perfect environment for you to cultivate it. After all, all you need to do is to enjoy what you do.
